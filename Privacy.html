Privacy Policy

Joy Log is a private journaling app for students. This policy explains what we collect, how we use it and what rights you have over your data. Written in plain language so you can actually understand it.

What we collect

When you create an account we collect your name, email address and password. When you use the app we store your journal entries, mood logs and the date each entry was written. That is all.

We do not collect your location, your contacts, your device information or anything else beyond what is listed above.

How we use your data

Your data is used for one purpose only — to run your Joy Log account and show you your own entries and mood history. Nothing else.

  • We never sell your data to anyone.
  • We never share your entries with any third party.
  • We never use your entries for advertising or AI training.
  • Our team cannot read your entries. They are encrypted and locked to your account only.

How we store your data

All journal entries are encrypted when they are sent to our servers and when they are stored. Your password is never stored in plain text. We use industry standard security practices throughout.

Your rights

You are always in control of your data. From the Settings page you can export all your entries at any time, delete individual entries, or delete your account and everything associated with it permanently.

Cookies & Changes

Joy Log only uses cookies to keep you logged in. We do not use tracking cookies, advertising cookies or any third party cookies.

If we ever update this policy we will notify you by email before the changes take effect. We will never make changes that reduce your privacy rights without giving you the chance to review them first.
